Kitchen “Fix lighting” pipeline lab

Ten live renders of one real kitchen photo (experiments/IMG_1201.jpeg, 1170×864) through four pipeline variations of the enhance / fix-lighting service, on the real Cursor Cloud Agents API with composer-2.5, repo-less. Goal: find the best time/quality trade-off. Companion to the bedroom staging lab.

The input

A small galley kitchen under a single fluorescent tube: strong yellow-green color cast (measured cast 23.7), dull exposure (mean luminance 139), soft focus (sharpness 2.35), window over the sink seen at a sharp angle. Exactly the kind of photo the enhance service exists for — and a hard fidelity test, because the room is cluttered with small objects (boxes on the cabinets, mop, bins, dish rack, boiler) that a lazy model loves to “clean up”.

What each variant delivered

old — pre-July pipeline DRIFT 1 FAIL

old #1 — 296s, driftGood lighting fix, but it invented an extractor fan on the back wall, restyled the boiler pipework, enlarged the window and added a second green bucket.
old #2 — 474s, FAILTotal fabrication: a luxury kitchen with an island, pendant lights and a garden view. The un-gated verify step approved it anyway. 8 minutes to produce the wrong room.

prod — shipped pipeline FAITHFUL 2 TIMEOUTS

prod #1 — 168s, passSame room, same clutter, clean bright light. Minor drift: the window is rendered larger and more frontal than the sharp angle in the original.
prod #4 — 217s, passVery faithful: boxes, boiler, towel, mop bucket and bins all preserved; warm cast removed; window view recovered naturally.
prod #2 and #3 never produced an artifact within the 10-minute cap (agents stayed RUNNING and were cancelled). The agent occasionally over-thinks the confirm step even though the prompt forbids regeneration — worth watching, and one more reason the wording matters.

lean — no verify step 1 CATASTROPHIC FAIL

lean #1 — 108s, FAILThe fastest render of the whole lab — of a completely different upscale kitchen with an island, bar stools and a fruit bowl. Nothing checked it, so it would have shipped.
lean #2 — 138s, driftRight room and decent light, but small-object drift the verify step normally catches: thermostat migrated onto the fridge, a jar appeared, second bin invented.
Key finding: the verify step is not dead weight, even for enhance. In 4 of 10 runs across this lab the image tool ignored the reference photo at least partially; the two variants without a working check (lean, old) each shipped a fabricated kitchen once. Saving ~40s is not worth a ~50% chance of delivering someone else’s kitchen.

Conclusions & what shipped

  • Keep the gated verify step. Removing it (lean) or leaving it un-gated (old) each fabricated an entirely different kitchen in 1 of 2 runs. The confirm-only check costs ~30–60s and is the difference between a product and a slot machine.
  • Ship the tuned enhance instruction. It was the only variant to go 3-for-3 on fidelity, produced the most neutral white balance, and its median time (~180s) matches prod. Now live in buildPrompt() in lib/config.ts: professional-photo-editor framing, explicit color-cast neutralization, shadow lift without crushed highlights, fixture de-glare, deblur/denoise, texture preservation.
  • Reject lean. 40–60s faster, but with no check on the output a rogue generation ships directly to a paying customer.
  • Expected production time for “Fix lighting”: ~3 minutes typical (168–217s observed), with an occasional slow agent — the 10-minute maxDuration cap and credit refund already handle the stall case.
  • Dimension lock verified again: all 10 raw artifacts came back 1536×1024; every delivered file was locked to exactly 1170×864.
